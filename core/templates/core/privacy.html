Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 17, 2026

What We Collect

When you use Bibliotype, we collect and process the following:

  • Reading data from your CSV upload — book titles, authors, ratings, dates read, and shelves. This data is used to generate your Reading DNA.
  • Account information (if you sign up) — display name, email address, and password (hashed and salted, never stored in plain text).
  • Usage analytics — page views, feature usage, and error tracking via PostHog (EU-hosted). We use this to improve the product.

How We Use Your Data

  • To generate your personalized Reading DNA dashboard and reader type.
  • To provide book recommendations based on readers with similar taste.
  • To compute community statistics and benchmarks (anonymized and aggregated).
  • To improve Bibliotype's features and fix bugs.

Anonymous Users

If you use Bibliotype without creating an account, your reading data is stored temporarily in a server-side session and an anonymous session record. This data is automatically deleted after 7 days. Before deletion, the session is anonymized — only aggregated genre and author distributions are retained (with no identifying information) to improve community recommendations.

Registered Users

If you create an account, your Reading DNA and book records are stored persistently so you can access your dashboard at any time. You can control your profile visibility:

  • Private (default) — only you can see your dashboard.
  • Public — anyone with your profile link can view your Reading DNA.
  • Recommendation visibility — you can opt out of appearing as a recommendation source for other users.

Third-Party Services

Bibliotype uses the following third-party services to function:

  • Google Gemini — to generate AI-powered reading vibes. Aggregated reading data (genres, reader type) is sent to generate personalized phrases. No personally identifiable information is included.
  • Open Library & Google Books APIs — to enrich book metadata (genres, ratings, page counts).
  • PostHog (EU-hosted) — for anonymous usage analytics and error tracking.

Data Security

All traffic is served over HTTPS. Passwords are hashed using Django's built-in PBKDF2 algorithm. Session data is stored server-side. We do not sell, share, or transfer your personal data to third parties for marketing purposes.

Your Rights

You can:

  • Make your profile private or public at any time from your dashboard.
  • Opt out of being shown as a recommendation source.
  • Request deletion of your account and all associated data by contacting us.

Contact

If you have questions about this policy or your data, open an issue on GitHub or reach out via the feedback form linked in the site banner.
